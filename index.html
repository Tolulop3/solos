SOLOS · PHASE 1 · UNDERSTANDING THE SYSTEM

What is SolOS actually doing?

SolOS is not a trading bot. It is a controlled exposure machine — a system that decides when to be in the market, how much to be exposed, and how fast to exit when things go wrong. This guide explains every score, every step, and what to watch for during calibration.

Core concept

The one idea that runs everything

Most trading systems ask: "what will the price do next?" SolOS asks a different question: "is this market environment safe enough to be active in right now?"

That shift changes everything. You are not trying to predict the future. You are trying to survive long enough to capture the rare moments when conditions are genuinely favourable — and compound your SOL through staking yield while you wait.

The core rule: SOL is always the denominator. Every token rotation starts in SOL, ends in SOL, and profits compound the SOL base. Tokens are tools. SOL is the truth asset.

Phase 1 has one job: validate that the system's logic actually matches reality. The thresholds — confidence above 60, sanity above 42, fragility weights — are all hypotheses right now. Phase 1 reveals which ones are wrong.

Score reference

The 7 scores — what each one measures

Every cycle computes 7 scores between 0 and 100. Here is what they mean and what to watch for.

REGIME PROBABILITY
0–100
How strongly the market signals one state. This is a probability weight, not a label. 72% expansion means the system is 72% confident — not that expansion is confirmed. Exposure scales with this number directly.
70+ — strong signal, full opportunity pool allowed
45–70 — moderate, reduced sizing
below 45 — unclear, system defaults to neutral
CONFIDENCE
0–100
How many input signals agree with each other. High confidence = price trend, volume, and volatility all point the same direction. Low confidence = they contradict — system should not act regardless of regime.
70+ — aligned, trading permitted with other gates passing
52–70 — partial agreement, reduced sizing
below 52 — contradicting signals, sanity gate will block
FRAGILITY
lower is better
Path memory — how damaged the system is from recent history. After losses, fragility rises. This makes the same market signal behave differently after a loss streak vs a win streak. It is path dependence made visible.
below 20 — clean history, normal exposure
20–50 — some damage, aggression reduced
above 50 — fragile, recovery mode likely active
TRADABILITY
0–100
Is the market liquid enough to enter and exit cleanly? Regime can say expansion while liquidity silently degrades. Tradability catches this independent of regime. It is the final protection against liquidity shock events.
70+ — liquid, clean exits expected
45–70 — thin, slippage risk elevated
below 45 — illiquid, no rotation regardless of regime
EXECUTION QUALITY
0–100
How clean is the execution environment right now? Even in a good regime, high volatility and thin spreads mean you cannot reliably enter or exit at expected prices. This score reduces trade frequency when conditions degrade.
70+ — clean, full frequency allowed
50–70 — degraded, reduce rotation count
below 50 — poor, hold only
AGGRESSION ×
0.4× – 1.2×
Performance-linked exposure scaling. Three consecutive wins unlock 1.2× sizing. Losses contract to 0.4–0.6×. The system earns the right to be more aggressive through demonstrated performance — not assumption.
1.2× — win streak, expanded pool
1.0× — neutral, standard sizing
0.4–0.6× — loss sequence, contracted
OBJECTIVE FUNCTION SCORE
what the system actually optimises
The single number defining whether the system achieves its goal. It is NOT maximum return. It penalises drawdowns heavily and penalises rough equity curves. A smooth 8% gain scores higher than a volatile 15% gain. This tells you if the system is compounding correctly — not just whether it is making money.
60+ — smooth compounding, system working as designed
40–60 — acceptable but equity curve is rough
below 40 — destroying value through volatility — recalibrate sizing down
Decision pipeline

The 7 steps — what happens each cycle

Every cycle runs these 7 steps in order. If any step blocks, the system halts there. You watch each step pass or block live on the dashboard.

01
Fragility — path memory check
Looks at recent history before anything else. How many consecutive losses? Is recovery mode active? The output modifies every subsequent step — after a drawdown, the same signal triggers smaller sizing and slower entry.
Watch: fragility above 50 — system is damaged, will restrict itself
02
Tradability — liquidity gate
Independent of regime. Even if the market looks great, if liquidity is thin the system cannot exit cleanly — so it should not enter. This is the final protection against liquidity shocks that cannot be modelled in advance.
Watch: tradability below 45 — blocks all rotation regardless of regime
03
Regime probabilities — continuous signal
Computes probability weights for expansion, neutral, and stress. Not a binary label. The probability feeds directly into allocation sizing — 70% expansion confidence allows larger opportunity pool than 50% expansion confidence.
Watch: regime flipping rapidly — signals transition risk, reduce sizing
04
Event-driven triggers — real-time safety
Separate from cycle timing. Watches for sudden volatility spikes and large fast price drops. These fire instantly — they do not wait for the next cycle. If EMERGENCY triggers, all tier 2 positions flatten and vault mode activates.
Watch: EMERGENCY flag turning red — overrides everything
05
Adaptive aggression cap — performance multiplier
Adjusts how much of the opportunity pool actually deploys based on recent performance. Three wins allow 1.2× sizing. Consecutive losses contract to 0.4–0.6×. Recovery mode caps at 0.5×. Emergency zeroes it.
Watch: aggression stuck below 0.6× — recovery mode protecting capital
06
Composite sanity check — final gate
Combines regime strength, tradability, execution quality, and fragility into one score. Catches interaction effects — each signal borderline acceptable but together they say do not trade. Formula: (regime×0.3) + (tradability×0.3) + (exec×0.2) − (fragility×0.2).
Watch: sanity passing but action feels wrong — this is your calibration signal
07
Execute or hold — logged with reason
In Phase 1 this logs a paper action. The decision JSON produced here is the exact format Phase 3 will consume for real Jupiter execution. Only the action handler changes. Every action logged with full reason in the trace.
Watch: system always STAKE — sanity threshold may be too high, try lowering to 0.36

CYCLES 1–10
Observation

Roadmap

What Phase 2 and 3 actually mean

PHASE 2 — WALLET READER
unlocks at LVL 1
Connects a read-only Solana wallet address. Capital state panel reads your actual SOL balance instead of paper assumptions. Regime and scores observe how your real portfolio weight behaves across market cycles. No execution — observation only.
Requires: 30 cycles complete + positive paper SOL growth
PHASE 3 — EXECUTION
unlocks at LVL 2
Execute button routes decision JSON to Jupiter API for real swaps. The 7-step pipeline and sanity gate are identical to Phase 1. Only the action handler changes — paper log becomes real transaction.
Requires: 60 cycles + 3 clean rotations + calibrated thresholds
